# NICE CG150 — Headaches in over 12s: diagnosis and management

**Source** : https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg150/chapter/Recommendations
**Version** : Last updated 2024 (originally 2012)
**Type** : NICE Clinical Guideline

---

## DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

### Tension-Type Headache
- **Pain location** : Bilateral
- **Quality** : Pressing/tightening (non-pulsating)
- **Intensity** : Mild or moderate
- **Effect on activities** : Not aggravated by routine daily living
- **Associated symptoms** : None
- **Duration** : 30 minutes to continuous
- **Episodic** : <15 days/month
- **Chronic** : ≥15 days/month for >3 months

### Migraine (with or without aura)
- **Pain location** : Unilateral OR bilateral
- **Quality** : Pulsating (throbbing or banging in young people aged 12-17)
- **Intensity** : Moderate or severe
- **Effect on activities** : Aggravated by, or causes avoidance of, routine activities
- **Associated symptoms** : Unusual sensitivity to light and/or sound OR nausea and/or vomiting
- **Duration** : 4-72 hours (adults) ; 1-72 hours (12-17 ans)
- **Episodic** : <15 days/month
- **Chronic** : ≥15 days/month with ≥8 migraine feature days for >3 months

### Migraine with Aura — critères
Neurological symptoms must be :
- Fully reversible
- Develop gradually over at least 5 minutes
- Last 5-60 minutes

**Typical aura** :
- Visual : flickering lights, spots or lines, partial loss of vision
- Sensory : pins and needles, numbness
- Speech disturbance

**Atypical aura → orientation neurologique** :
- Motor weakness
- Double vision
- Monocular visual symptoms
- Poor balance
- Decreased consciousness level

### Cluster Headache (algie vasculaire de la face)
- **Pain location** : Unilateral (around eye, above eye, side of head/face)
- **Quality** : Sharp, boring, burning, throbbing or tightening
- **Intensity** : Severe or very severe
- **Associated symptoms** (SAME side) : red/watery eye, nasal congestion/runny nose, swollen eyelid, forehead/facial sweating, constricted pupil and/or drooping eyelid
- **Duration** : 15-180 minutes
- **Episodic** : 1×/2j à 8×/j avec >1 mois pain-free
- **Chronic** : idem avec <1 mois pain-free en 12 mois

### Menstrual-related migraine
Predominantly between 2 days before and 3 days after start of menstruation, in ≥2/3 consecutive cycles. Diagnosis = headache diary over ≥2 cycles.

### Medication Overuse Headache
Alert if headache developed/worsened during :
- Triptans, opioids, ergots, combination analgesics : ≥10 days/month for 3+ months
- Paracetamol, aspirin, NSAID (alone/combined) : ≥15 days/month for 3+ months

## RED FLAGS — secondary headache (1.1.1-1.1.2)

### Evaluate / refer if :
- Worsening headache with fever
- Sudden-onset headache reaching maximum intensity within 5 minutes ("coup de tonnerre")
- New-onset neurological deficit
- New-onset cognitive dysfunction
- Change in personality
- Impaired consciousness level
- Recent head trauma (typically ≤3 months)
- Headache triggered by cough, valsalva or sneeze
- Headache triggered by exercise
- Orthostatic headache (changes with posture)
- Symptoms suggestive of giant cell arteritis (Horton)
- Symptoms/signs of acute narrow angle glaucoma
- Substantial change in headache characteristics

### Additional red flags
- Compromised immunity (HIV, immunosuppressive drugs)
- Age <20 with malignancy history
- History of brain-metastasizing malignancy
- Vomiting without obvious cause

---

## IMAGING

"Do not refer people diagnosed with tension-type headache, migraine, cluster headache or medication overuse headache for neuroimaging solely for reassurance."

For suspected brain tumours : refer per NICE NG12 (suspected cancer).

## ACUTE TREATMENT

### Tension-Type Headache
- Aspirin, paracetamol, or NSAID
- Aspirin **CI <16 years** (Reye's syndrome)
- **Do not offer opioids**

### Migraine Acute Treatment

**Combination therapy (1st line)** :
- Oral triptan + NSAID, OR oral triptan + paracetamol
- Ages 12-17 : prefer nasal triptan

**Monotherapy** :
- Oral triptan, NSAID, aspirin (900 mg), or paracetamol

**Triptan selection** :
- Start with lowest acquisition cost ; if ineffective, try alternative

**Anti-emetics** :
- Consider even in absence of nausea/vomiting

**Non-oral options** if oral/nasal ineffective :
- Non-oral metoclopramide or prochlorperazine
- Add non-oral NSAID or triptan

**Contraindicated** :
- **Do NOT offer ergots or opioids for acute migraine**

### Cluster Headache Acute Treatment
- **Oxygen and/or subcutaneous or nasal triptan**
- Oxygen : 100% O₂ at flow ≥12 L/min via non-rebreathing mask with reservoir bag
- **Do NOT offer paracetamol, NSAIDs, opioids, ergots or oral triptans**

## PROPHYLACTIC TREATMENT

### Migraine Prophylaxis (1st line options)
**Consider** : Propranolol, topiramate, or amitriptyline

- **Propranolol** : caution in depression (self-harm)
- **Topiramate** : CI in pregnancy/women of childbearing age without Pregnancy Prevention Programme
- **Amitriptyline** : follow NG215 for AD safe prescribing

**Do not offer gabapentin** for migraine prophylaxis.

### Acupuncture
If 1st line fails : up to 10 sessions over 5-8 weeks.

### Riboflavin
400 mg once daily — may reduce migraine frequency/intensity.

### CGRP inhibitors (≥4 days/month, after ≥3 preventives failed)
- Atogepant (oral)
- Eptinezumab (IV)
- Fremanezumab (SC)
- Erenumab 140 mg (SC)
- Galcanezumab (SC)
- Rimegepant (oral) for episodic 4-14 days/month

### Botulinum Toxin Type A
Chronic migraine after ≥3 preventives failed AND medication overuse managed.

### Review
Review prophylaxis 3-6 months after starting.

**Stop CGRP inhibitor** if frequency reduction <50% (episodic) or <30% (chronic) after 12 weeks.

### Cluster Headache Prophylaxis
- **Verapamil** (1st line during bout) — specialist advice, ECG monitoring
- Pregnancy : specialist advice

### Combined Hormonal Contraceptives in Migraine with Aura
**Do NOT routinely offer** — increased stroke risk.

### Menstrual-Related Migraine
- Frovatriptan 2.5 mg BID on expected migraine days
- Zolmitriptan 2.5 mg 2-3×/day on expected migraine days

## MEDICATION OVERUSE HEADACHE — management

1. **Withdraw** overused medication completely for ≥1 month, stopped **abruptly** (not gradually)
2. Warn : symptoms likely to worsen short-term before improving ; withdrawal symptoms possible
3. Consider concurrent prophylaxis for underlying primary headache
4. **Do not routinely** offer inpatient withdrawal
5. Consider specialist referral if : strong opioid users, comorbidities, repeated failed attempts
6. Review diagnosis and management 4-8 weeks after withdrawal start

---

## ASSESSMENT TOOLS

### Headache diary
**Minimum 8 weeks** recording :
- Frequency, duration, severity
- Associated symptoms
- All prescribed/OTC medications
- Precipitants
- Relationship to menstruation

---

## REFERRAL

- First cluster headache bout : discuss imaging with GP-with-special-interest or neurologist
- Giant cell arteritis symptoms
- Atypical aura
- Red flags
- Specialist advice : verapamil initiation, failed verapamil, pregnancy cluster, failed CGRP
